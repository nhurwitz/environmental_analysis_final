Hong Kong

Hong Kong Overview

  • Hong kong has a population of over 7 million. Each day there are over 12 million passenger journeys on public transport.
  • 90% of daily journeys in Hong Kong are public transport (highest rate in the world)
  • Because of Hong Kong's steep, hilly terrain, massive covered outdoor escalator/walkways have been implemented. Daily traffic on these exceeds 85,000
  • The Massive Transit Railway (MTR) is an electric railway that carries about 4.4 million passenger trips per day
  • Additionally, Hong Kong boasts double-decker tramways, funicular railways, automated people-movers, buses, and cross-boundary trains
  • In 2005, there were only 18,138 taxis in Hong Kong
  • Cars are subjected to a 35-100% registration tax; parking is tough and petroleum is heavily taxed. As of 2009, there were 584,000 licensed vehicles in Hong Kong. Thatâ€™s about 1 car for every 12 Hong Kong residents.
  • Railway transport: energy use = 1/22 of private car. Public transport: energy use = 1/7 of private car
  • Some Hong Kong transportation figures: A private car passenger emits 170g of carbon dioxide per km, a bus passenger emits 34g, while a railway passenger emits 2.8g.
